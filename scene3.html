Highest Emitting Countries

Cause and Effect of high CO2 emission

This bar chart visualizes CO2 emissions by country for 2019. The bar lengths indicate emission levels, with longer bars showing higher emissions.

🌍 The chart highlights the top emitters: China, the United States, India, and Russia.

  • 🇨🇳 China: The largest emitter due to extensive industry and coal use. It faces air pollution and extreme weather like heatwaves and flooding.
  • 🇺🇸 United States: High per capita emissions linked to hurricanes, wildfires, and droughts, with significant economic and social impacts.
  • 🇮🇳 India: Rapid industrial growth leads to rising emissions. Faces heatwaves, erratic monsoons, and rising sea levels affecting agriculture and water supply.
  • 🇷🇺 Russia: Major energy producer with significant emissions. Experiences permafrost thawing, shifting agricultural zones, and increased wildfires.

🔍 These emissions contribute to global warming and climate change, leading to rising temperatures, changing weather patterns, and severe weather events. Urgent action is needed to mitigate these impacts.
